ABOUT ME

Short Bio

I’m Nathan Tu. I came to Australia in 1994 from Vietnam at the age of 18 as an international student learning English. Fast forward, I became Australian citizen in 2005 and worked in various financial companies for the last 20 years, ranging from big banks such as Westpac and Commonwealth to boutique firms and ran my own mortgage business until I sold it last year.

How I got into tech

My aspiration for getting into tech coming from my philosophy of exploring new opportunities and challenging my limits. I wanted to do something new but also utilising my existing skills in finance. By looking at how my country Vietnam has grown and until today, only 7 fintech startup companies have been rolled out. With a population of 100m people, I could sense that there would be huge potential in getting into tech. Hence, there Global Hunch was born. The name came from taking my intuition and go global, again “exploring new opportunities and challenge my limits”. We now focus on two key areas in our business: a marketplace for fitness and a fintech app for mortgages. With a difficulty of convincing couple of tech friends to join our business and take out equity, I decided to take on the skill and knowledge of how to program. And here I am, learning another language from scratch at Coder Academy.
